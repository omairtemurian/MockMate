MOCKMATE — DELIVERABLE 3: USER EMPATHY MAP
================================================================

User Persona:
    Name:       Amir
    Profile:    Mid-level developer, 3 years of experience,
                applying for his first senior role at a tech company

----------------------------------------------------------------
THINKS & FEELS
----------------------------------------------------------------
- "What if they ask something I don't know?"
- "I know the answers but I freeze up when speaking out loud."
- "Prep tools feel robotic and generic — they're not asking ME
  about MY experience."
- Anxious about sounding confident and structured under pressure
- Worried the questions won't match what this company actually asks
- Feels guilty for not practising more, but doesn't know how

----------------------------------------------------------------
SAYS & DOES
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Reads the job description 3 times and highlights keywords
- Googles "top interview questions for [role]" and gets generic lists
- Asks ChatGPT to quiz him — but types the answers, doesn't speak them
- Books 1 session with a career coach then cancels because it is $200
- Practises answers in the mirror but gets no feedback
- Talks through answers with a friend who has no industry context

----------------------------------------------------------------
PAINS
----------------------------------------------------------------
- No realistic, voice-based practice available without spending money
- Generic questions don't match the actual job description
- Human feedback is expensive and hard to schedule
- Cannot hear how he sounds or know if he speaks too long / too short
- No way to know which answers were weak and why
- Has to wait until the real interview to find out if he's ready

----------------------------------------------------------------
GAINS (what they actually want)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Hear real questions spoken out loud — like a real interview
- Answer by speaking, not typing — to practice the actual skill
- Know exactly which answers scored low and why
- Get one actionable tip per answer to improve
- Build confidence through repetition before the real thing
- A PDF report they can review the night before the interview
